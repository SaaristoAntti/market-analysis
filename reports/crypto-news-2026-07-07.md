**1. Glamsterdam ("Amsterdam") Hard Fork Goes Live**
On July 6, 2026, Ethereum activated the Glamsterdam upgrade — a dual-layer hard fork updating both the Execution Layer (Amsterdam) and Consensus Layer (Gloas). The upgrade introduces Enshrined Proposer-Builder Separation (ePBS) and Block-Level Access Lists (BALs), enabling parallel transaction processing and paving the way for throughput toward 10,000 TPS. It is one of the most significant Ethereum upgrades since the Merge.
- [Glamsterdam — ethereum.org](https://ethereum.org/roadmap/glamsterdam/)
- [BingX Guide](https://bingx.com/en/learn/article/what-is-the-ethereum-glamsterdam-upgrade-gloas-amsterdam-hard-fork)

**2. Vitalik Buterin Unveils "Lean Ethereum" Roadmap**
Also on July 6, Buterin published a new "Lean Ethereum" roadmap — a 3-4 year strategic plan prioritising quantum resistance and protocol-level privacy. The plan builds on the Ethereum Foundation's earlier "Strawmap" which outlined seven planned network forks through 2029, targeting ~10,000 L1 TPS, millions of L2 TPS, sub-20-second finality, post-quantum cryptography, and native shielded ETH transfers.
